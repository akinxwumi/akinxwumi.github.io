# XXVIII

### Reflecting on the period between January 1, 2022 — January 31, 2023

Writing this has been a struggle for the last two months or so, and it wasn’t until yesterday morning when I reminded myself of why I do this, that I found some impetus. This is not a vainglorious project. On the contrary, it is mainly an attempt to preserve my mental state over a yearly transition so that when I look back, I will have a film of data to go through. Thinking about it, not a lot of significant things happened to me personally during this period, especially in the early parts (January–June), which were mostly concentrated around my work with Helicarrier and also settling in Yaba after relocating from Arepo into a horrendous house.

A ship in the harbor is safe, but that’s not why ships are built.

On June 20, I began working on Abacus (a new venture for the umpteenth time) with some of my former team members, and despite the scars from previous experiences, relapsed from gainful employment! Alas, I apparently couldn’t run away from myself, or at least this is the way I rationalized it. Some people sing, and others dance. Some people write, and others talk. Some play while others watch. I build products (tools) and try to solve problems with them. I couldn’t run away from my shadow.

Will this finally be my waterloo? I don’t know yet.

Here are some of the things I learned during this period:

- The world is cold and adulthood is to a large extent, a scam. People in our generation are highly opportunistic. Relationships are becoming more and more transactional. A lot of people are not necessarily interested in you and are just fishing.
- How do you cope with rejection? How do you embrace a constant "no” from almost everyone and still continue to try to recruit and sell your vision without losing enthusiasm (as if you do, then your pitch won’t be as convincing)?
- Humans are just too complicated; the more you look, the less you know, so it is better to just scratch the surface in most instances. The best is to be the focal point around which all other humans revolve. This way, you can dictate your environment and relationship conditions with other humans.
- Tension is good. Lean into it. Embrace it.
- The first step in solving a puzzle is conceiving a plan. a picture of what the solution looks like.
