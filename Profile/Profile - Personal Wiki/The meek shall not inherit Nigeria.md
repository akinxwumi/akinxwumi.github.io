# The meek shall not inherit Nigeria

In a world where politics and power struggles are constantly at play, one man dedicated his life to uncovering the secrets of cooperation and competition. Robert Axelrod was a brilliant political scientist whose groundbreaking research on the dynamics of human behavior has proven invaluable time and time again.

In his experiments, Axelrod created a virtual game that pitted individuals against each other in a battle of self-interest versus cooperation. The rules were simple, yet the implications were profound: to win, players had to find the optimal balance between cooperation and competition.

Axelrod invited researchers from around the world to submit their strategies for playing this game, and what followed was a tournament that would go down in history. The most successful players were those who followed a strategy known as "Tit for Tat": they started by cooperating with their opponents and then mirrored their opponents' previous moves. If their opponent cooperated, they cooperated in return. If their opponent cheated, they would cheat in return. The worst performers were the suckers who always cooperated, leaving themselves vulnerable to exploitation by cheaters who did not hesitate to take advantage.

But it was the ruthless players who were most surprising. They started out strong, exploiting the suckers and succeeding in the short term. However, as the experiment progressed, they found themselves facing only other ruthless players and Tit for Tat players. They soon realized that they were losing in the long term, as their strategy was unable to foster any cooperation or build any trust among the players.

The lesson here is clear. In order to build a successful society, we need to cooperate with each other and punish those who cheat. This is particularly relevant in the context of the recent Nigerian presidential election, in which a party and their accomplices blatantly cheated. But this will not happen, and they will continue to cheat until we either punish them or perish!

It's easy to feel powerless in the face of such injustice, but Axelrod's research shows that there is a way to fight back. By working together, punishing those who cheat, and fostering an environment of trust and cooperation, we can build a better society for ourselves and for future generations.
