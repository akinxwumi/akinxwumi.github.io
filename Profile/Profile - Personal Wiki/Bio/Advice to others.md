# Advice to others

I am not sure I am in a position to give advice as it is still potentially early days for me. But if I am to, it will be:

- Be a net producer. Don’t just consume, produce. Think of IMPACT. Be ashamed to die until you have won some victory for humanity.
- Also, focus: intensity mostly always trumps extensity. The caveat is that this is obviously relative to the amount of capital you have. The main thing is to not over-extend yourself.
- What you can do is limitless. People that have changed the world, I believe, are no better than you and I. They just got the kind of leverage Archimedes asked for through sheer hard work, luck, persistence, and more. For instance, there are three primary colours, eight musical notes, nine digits, and two (0&1) binary digits in the case of computer systems, but look at what their combinations can achieve. The trick is to find ways to combine basic things to create even greater things.
- Have (cultivate) high agency. Cultivate a bias for action.
- Have skin in the game. Be a missionary (deeply care and believe) in anything you do, versus a mercenary. Be independent-minded (contrarian).
- Read The Almanack of Naval Ravikant and follow him. It contains great mental models and frameworks for the modern age. Levers for the mind.
- Pick up critical thinking tools as your life is a cornucopia of decisions. Hence, you need to have robust decision-making processes (great judgement). Frameworks, mental models, quotes, metaphors, etc. Remember to always use Naval’s razor when you have to make a difficult choice between two options.
