Embassy and Proof of Funds

When the British Raj asked the Indians to kill poisonous cobras, it gave rise to the cobra effect. Proof of funds is another such policy as well. It has been completely hacked, at least in Nigeria, and I wouldn’t blame anyone who fools the British, Canadian, etc. governments in this way. It is the path of least resistance, and as we know, we are naturally configured to follow this path.

How do these governments expect lower- and middle-class African citizens who are desirous of migrating and have resorted to schooling abroad to pony up this exorbitant sum of money? The only way is to either steal it or lie about having it. Good luck finding out.

I will propose that the UK, Canadian government, etc., instead of deceiving themselves or, worse, making the study requirements more stringent, create a system that encourages people to study but also takes into consideration that they won’t be able to instantly fund their studies [1]. After all, they have a credit system specifically for this purpose in their countries for their citizens. Why not extend something similar to “academic” migrants? They will most likely create more value in your economy anyway, as, let’s be honest, most people “Japaing” right now don’t intend to come back, at least not immediately.

Notes

[1] There are scholarship programs, but these are not robust enough to accommodate the vast majority of the people who are at the center of the normal distribution.
