Fighting your own biochemistry

This is a losing battle. Our body’s biochemistry is incredibly powerful, making it extremely hard to resist its innate urges. Sooner or later, it gets what it wants. This is evident, for instance, in honey-trapping exploits, where people are seduced into compromising situations by attractive individuals against their better judgment. Even those with a high level of self-control can be susceptible to it, as our biochemistry can override our rational selves.

It is crucial, then, to be conscious of this bug in our nature. If we find ourselves in a situation that is likely to trigger our biological urges, it may be wise to flee from the situation rather than try to resist it [1]. As Warren Buffett once said, “If you put a police car on anyone’s tail for 500 miles, they are gonna get a ticket.” No matter how strong our self-control is, it is not always enough. We are all susceptible to temptation (i.e., an adverse stimulus that strongly appeals to our innate biological desires) and will lapse if we are exposed to it for long enough.

Notes

[1] Of course, this is easier said than done.
