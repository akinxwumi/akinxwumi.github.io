Akin Wumi

On founding, founders and foundations

I just read the “Founding Brothers” and I now hold the founding fathers of the USA nation in higher esteem than previously.

Their brilliance, dexterity, and foresight provided the bedrock for the American nation to thrive, and this is, incidentally, what most of the African nations severely lacked, unfortunately, at those auspicious founding moments post-independence.

For those that had really capable and visionary founders e.g. Patrice Lumumba of Congo, their ingenuity was frustrated by the exigencies of the competing interests of the USA and USSR alongside neocolonialism.

If only their brilliance were more evenly distributed in the leadership class as at the time, maybe, just maybe, their infant nations would have been able to withstand the inevitable geopolitical storm and chart courses that would let them blossom similarly.
