Reflecting on the period between Nov 8, 2020 — November 8, 2021

It was a very tough year chai 😭. Apart from the days of running Token Nigeria, the middle of this period was probably the nadir of my life so far. Sometime between September 2020 and December 2020, I and wisdom worked and iterated on the initial version of Bookmakr - an odds comparison tool that morphed into a booking code aggregation and conversion feed - then we started 2021 by making it publicly available but within 4 months, we had to shut it down even though it was one of the most difficult things we had ever built.

🥅 The goal post kept shifting

From a financial perspective, it was a promising product! In fact, we had modest traction (over 150K page views and 7K visitors in the first 3 months) but it wasn’t a problem space that resonated with us. We started out wanting to build a tool that finds arbitrage opportunities in the sports betting market as a personal side-hustle only to be confronted with the reality of the widespread havoc gambling is causing especially in Africa. We felt the need to stem this tide and we briefly tried in our own way but alas, a new type of staking model that is less pervasive and equally attractive will have to be fostered to disrupt the bookmakers which will require people with the right skillsets, passion, and resolve. The team behind this is doing something that is noteworthy in the space.

💡 Startups will humble you

So after this debacle, I was understandably disillusioned and needed a break. I went completely offline for approx. 4 months between June and October to contemplate what had happened. There is nothing to write about this period except that it was sombre for the most part: a moment of existential dread as I wondered where the last ~8 years (2014–2021) of my life had gone as even though I actively worked on Forfinders, Hifofin, Portalng, Token Nigeria, Trivia9ja, Nairalife, and then Bookmakr, it appeared I’ve not accomplished anything tangible with over 70K hours of existence except perhaps building a mediocre skillset!

It is a slip, not a fall — Abraham Lincoln

Asides from brooding, I also read a few books, hung out more with friends, improved on my product management and chess skills, reconstructed my mental models and frameworks, reassessed some of my ethos including my stance on gainful employment and also heuristics for evaluating whether a startup idea is worth pursuing. If there is a positive from this chapter, it is that my prefrontal cortex seemed to have evolved and become more sophisticated as my process of making decisions has gotten more rigorous.

L — R Willams and Akin

In October, I was co-opted into an interesting team that wants to financially connect Africans anywhere in the world to the global economy by leveraging crypto because their mission deeply resonates with my Pan-African ideals, and in early November on my birthday, I was with my kith and kin and even though we couldn’t do much but enjoy our company, it was nonetheless worthwhile as I’ve not for instance seen my mum in 5+ long years which was a big plus.

This will probably be the only episode of this nature I will ever write about so it is pertinent that I note my main takeaway which is that life is a complex system and therefore very turbulent. One day you’re at the top of the world, the next you’re at the bottom, and vice versa so one should always strive to remain unwavering by its constant flux.
