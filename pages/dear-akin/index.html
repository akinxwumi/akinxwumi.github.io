Dear Akin

I have been thinking about you and having considered your antecedents and also the actions you have taken recently despite our numerous admonitions, I have decided to write this to you as a last recourse with the hope that it serves you well in the course of your life that is just beginning.

Below, I highlighted 5 basic life principles some of which I have had to learn the hard way, and even though they are by no means exhaustive, I firmly believe that if you adopt them, you will stand in good stead regardless of the circumstances at any point in your life onwards.

1. Always think long term

Don’t trade away long-term callings to satisfy short-term longings. Do you remember the legendary story of Esau in the bible? Be strategic and less impulsive especially when you have to make non-reversible decisions or take actions with long-term implications. Always consider the worst-case scenario and ask yourself whether it is something you can accept.

2. Incentives are everything

No matter what your and others’ true intentions are, incentives mostly dictate human actions so if you desire an outcome, create an incentive structure that encourages it!

3. Choose smart over cunning

Sometimes you have to be cunning to be smart but if you have to make a choice, always defer to smartness over cunning as it usually pays more on a long enough timescale.

4. Have skin in the game

Play mostly games that you’ve some kind of exposure to and only play with those that have a similar level of skin as you do. Take chances on people, tools, and ideas as without risk, there can be no reward but avoid those that can cause you intolerable ruin. Never play negative and zero-sum games even if you have an edge except either winning is certain, the cost is insignificant or you have no choice. Play infinite (iterated), positive-sum games especially those that are asymmetric instead.

5. Leverage Compounding

Compound interest is the most powerful force in the universe and if you understand it, you have discovered God.

To leverage it, follow these steps:

  1. Understand yourself. What are your strengths and weaknesses? What is your calling? Define your passion and talents. Find your base (niche) i.e. whatever gives you an edge over others and your growth rate will be far superior.

  2. Work very hard to acquire capital (financial, social, labour, intellectual, media, etc.), understand how they work, and apply it to your base.

  3. Be disciplined and patient enough to allow time to work its magic.

  4. Constantly redefine your base.

Lastly, please note that long-term thinking is the foundation upon which the other axioms lay, and never forget these favourite sayings of mum:

  1. As you lay your bed, you shall lie on it.

  2. Cut your coat according to your cloth.

  3. Success is the best perfume.

Yours lovingly,

Akin Wumi
