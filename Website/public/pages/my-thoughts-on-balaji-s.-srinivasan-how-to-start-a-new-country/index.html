My Thoughts On Balaji S. Srinivasan How To Start A New Country

Published on April 14, 2023.

I’ve been engrossed by the concept of a network state from an article I recently read by Balaji S. Srinivasan on how to start a new country. As a Nigerian who like many others is barely surviving within a failing state and whose nationality is a byproduct of colonialism, the possibility that I and countless others can escape the Nigerian contraption in a pragmatic way and be part of a thriving, supranational state that transcends physical boundaries is irresistible and here are my thoughts:

Firstly, building a new country devoid of historical constraint is great but the prospect of attainable citizenship for anyone is even more appealing in my opinion. One’s citizenship shouldn’t primarily be determined by virtue of birth and anyone should be able to win the ovarian lottery.

Secondly, I agree that the most practical way to start a new country is to proceed cloud-first, land last but will it compete with or complement the current types of nations? If it’s a competition, can it be at least in some dimensions 10X better for a significant number of people than the current alternatives especially those in the developed countries with relatively good systems of governance?

I am also curious about the potential economic models, recruitment strategies, culture among many other things for such a country that weren’t addressed in the article:

  1. What are the incentives for members to join especially the early adopters?
  2. How do we attract both mercenaries and missionaries?
  3. How do we get builders and keep bad actors at bay?
  4. Organizing the internal economy around remote work is a bit vague, does it mean for people to be able to participate in the internal economy of the state, they have to work for the state or its members?
  5. If there is Minimum Necessary Innovation, what is the corresponding Minimum Viable Concept? Is it a virtual community with rules of engagement as suggested in the article and if so, how do we organically grow our culture in such an enclave?
  6. What are examples of individual initiatives that might be useful?

In conclusion, if a network state as delineated by Balaji comes to fruition, the possibilities are endless and I can’t wait for him to posit how we might go about this and consequently start showing my own individual initiative.
